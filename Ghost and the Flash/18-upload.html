However she came to be, she is a powerful guardian of sorts; she guards boxes and containers of… Danny isn’t sure what exactly, but all he knows is that they are artifacts of power that should not fall into the wrong hands. Pandora had told him a couple of stories of what has happened when they do fall into the wrong hands.

A ghost with powerful artifacts and knowledge is bound to help him meet the ghost of a famous scientist – or point him in the right direction to do that. Maybe.

Danny flew around aimlessly in the Ghost Zone, until he found Sidney’s lair. A sense of direction in the Ghost Zone was difficult, in that lairs and floating islands weren’t tethered to spots in reality, but following some invisible orbit, as if they were planets in an unknown system. Danny navigated using relative landmarks, which seemed to group certain ectoplasmic bodies together, and found Pandora’s island some ways off.

Her lair was a town on an island – fields, homes, roads. If Danny had to guess, the architecture was probably from a certain era of Greek history. Many spirits wandered around the ghost town, continuing their lifestyle even in death, but Pandora herself was in the Acropolis, overlooking the town itself. Danny flew to the front and knocked at the door. She answered after the first knock.

“Hello, Phantom,”

“Hi, Pandora,”

Good, straight to the point and no riddles yet.

He’d only visited Pandora once before, when he was looking for a place to hide Eragon’s necklace – the artifact that transformed the ghost prince into a powerful dragon – and Pandora led him around in riddles before agreeing to store the necklace for safe keeping.

“So, I need to ask – did someone steal Eragon’s necklace from your vaults? He attacked Amity Park a few months ago in his dragon form,” Danny shook the thermos in his hand, containing the thermos that had said necklace. While Eragon was returned to the ghost zone, Danny had since kept that necklace inside a Fenton Thermos – he had,... procrastinated on bringing the necklace back to Pandora, since he wasn’t sure it wouldn’t be stolen from her again.

Pandora gave a cryptic smile.

“I owed a favor to a friend; I cannot say much about him. You will meet him soon, though from his perspective, the meeting has already happened.”

Ah, there were the riddles. But Danny was more experienced with riddles now than he was before!

"A meeting with another ghost that will happen soon, and has already happened… is what I came to ask you about, actually,” Danny responded. The necklace wasn’t the only thing he came to Pandora about.

“What do you mean?”

“I mean, people from the past who are ghosts now – their past is my future when I learn about them and talk to them, in some roundabout way.” A little bit of redirection, but it was working in his favor. “Can I visit any of those ghosts? Or talk to them?”

“Ah,” Pandora’s confusion eased out. “I was forewarned about this. Follow me.”

She heads deeper into her domain, and Danny follows. Something about flipping the riddle back on his head to reveal the solution worked last time, and this time it coincided with his goal, so he must have solved Pandora’s riddle properly this time – maybe.

They float through a hallway lined with pedestals that hold various vases and boxes, each one decorated to describe the artifact they contain.

“To some degree, you can visit other ghosts – if you know about them, that is,” They stop in front of one pedestal, and Pandora pulls out a keyboard from a nondescript box. “And you cannot know about ghosts without searching. This tool can help with that.”

Pandora was letting him use an ancient artifact? And that ancient artifact was a keyboard?

“What? Are you sure about this?” Danny was flabbergasted. “Keyboards are a modern invention, aren’t they? This can’t be an ancient artifact!”

“I never said powerful artifacts were ancient – you just assumed that,” Pandora chuckled. “Besides, this particular artifact conforms its interface to what its user is familiar with. It’s a search tome, that can tell you information about ghosts.”

Danny rested his hands on the floating keyboard, and a holographic screen popped up in front of him. A ghost computer, Technus would be frothing at the mouth if he got his hands on this. Okay, focus! Danny wanted to search up ghosts of famous scientists, that’s what he had come here to ask Pandora about to begin with. Okay.

Danny typed in “Marie Curie”, hit the enter key, and found a single search result. He clicked on it using the trackpad on the keyboard, and it opened a single image. He stared a bit at the symbols and images, trying to understand what he saw.

“That’s a map, isn’t it?” Danny asked. “A map to her… lair?”

“That it is,” Pandora confirmed.

Danny looked back at the map – it looked pretty far from here, wherever “here” was, and he didn’t want to leave the keyboard either. He tried another search term: “Nikola Tesla” – the search result showed him the location to (and a picture of) Technus’s lair, which he had passed by on his way to Pandora’s.

“Not all ghosts retain their original sense of self, do they?” Danny asked.

“No they don’t,” Pandora responded. “Many can get corrupted, just as this one you searched, and each ghost has varying levels of awareness of their state of being. There are even some who do not know they have died, and are reliving their favorite memories of their life on a loop within their lair, to come to terms with their deaths”

Would that be Danny one day, when he goes from half ghost to full ghost? He shook that thought out of his mind and hit the back button immediately.

Danny could theoretically search up the ghosts of any famous figure in history, and interview them for… anything. Just like Valerie thought. Assuming the ghosts hadn’t been corrupted, still retained their sense of self, and were nice enough to let Danny interview them in some way.

“If this is meant to be a search tome, it should be able to tell me about ghosts even if I don’t know their names, right?” Danny mused. “Like a search engine. Like a ghost google.”

“Something like that,” Pandora affirmed.

His fingertips itched with power; the possibilities of which ghosts he could learn from, the potential to have all of human history as an asset was intoxicating. It was one thing to interview a famous president from hundreds of years ago, it was another to be tutored by a famous scientist. Oh, Barry would be so jealous.

He entered a new search term: “famous scientists who died in the past 30 years” and hit enter. He didn’t want to scare a potential ghost tutor away with culture shock, so it would probably be best to stick to recently deceased scientists. This time, the search results returned a list of names he’d have to sift through. Some of the names were familiar – he’d heard his parents discuss some of these names in passing, while discussing breakthroughs in adjacent scientific fields. He scrolled through the list, and paused.

There was one name in the search results that did not belong.

“Hey Pandora,” the large ghost was idling by, supervising Danny’s use of the keyboard. He couldn’t believe that Pandora was just letting him use it, it was incredibly powerful.

“What happens when there’s multiple people with the same name in the search results?”

“That happens more often than you think,” Pandora responded. “Have you seen the British royalty? They keep reusing the same names! If you ever search them up, you have to be careful that you have the right person. Humans reuse names all the time.”

That…

“So if someone who’s de– someone who’s a ghost has the same name as someone who’s alive… it’s entirely possible that’s a coincidence, right?”

“It could be,”

Danny clicked the suspicious search result, opening a map to their lair. It was… far.

“Do you think I could go visit this one?” Danny pointed.

“Sure,” Pandora took off one of her necklaces and gave it to Danny. “So long as you wear this, you can teleport yourself back here instantly.”

“And you’re just gonna let me go and visit another ghost’s lair? No riddle?”

“See, I’d rather have brought the ghost here, rather than send you to them,” Pandora mused. “But that would require summoning a ghost, which is not something I, or any artifact, can do; only the Ghost King can do that. So the next best thing is for you to go to them. Besides, you learn so much more when you visit a person in their homes instead of inviting them out.”

Danny couldn’t help but feel like this entire visit was a riddle thus far, one he hadn’t figured out yet. He sighed; at least she wasn’t stopping him.

He moved the pointer to click on the ghost’s lair, and a portal opened up next to him. Danny clutched the amulet Pandora gave him, and went through it, arriving outside the ghost lair of one Dr. Harrison Wells.

Please please please let this be a coincidence, Danny thought.

The lair was a nondescript two story building atop a floating island, the kind Danny wouldn’t glance at twice while flying through the Zone. The front door was open, so he went inside. Inside was a… not a lab, but a research abode of sorts; papers and books haphazardly placed across multiple desks and the floor. Some beakers on one of the tables. The most eye-catching part of the room was the giant bubble of ectoplasm floating in the middle.

“What… is that?”

Danny leaned in close to the ecto-bubble. He could… see something inside. It was tinted green, but he could see people inside, talking to each other. Were they… trapped inside? Was the ecto-bubble a solid shield? Maybe he could free them. Danny touched the surface of the bubble – it was semi solid, but his hand passed through.

The noise of shattering glass behind him caught his attention.

“Oh no! Don’t touch that!” A ghost in a lab coat came into the room. “I was researching that! It might suck you in!”

Danny felt a tug and couldn’t stop himself from getting pulled inside, almost like the bubble was eating him. He yelled and reached out for the other ghost, their hands almost touching before Danny was swallowed whole.

-

The first sight Danny could see a younger Wells, a newly minted PhD, sitting at a cafeteria table at what Danny presumed was inside his university. He looked just like the picture that was on his Wikipedia page. He was poring over his blueprints with a few white coated colleagues of his. Everyone was eating slices of pizza. Danny walked over to them, peeking over a few shoulders to look at the blueprints. He saw a familiar logo on the bottom right.

“Star Labs,” Danny noted.

“Yes, for cutting edge research into the future of physics!” the ghost of young-looking Wells responded. “You don’t think it sounds cheesy do you? Tess thinks it sounds cheesy.”

Danny was caught off guard.

“I… don’t think it’s cheesy, actually,” Danny responded. “I mean, I like astronomy so I might be a little biased here.”

“Wonderful! Thank you for breaking the tie on our vote!” The ghost of Wells shook Danny’s hand. “You saved it from being named ‘Central Labs’ which is honestly just lame,”

“It’s not lame! It’s –”

Ghost-Wells’s peers start to argue over each other, and a plate of pizza gets knocked over to the ground. Danny bends down to pick it up – surprised that he can interact with it – and gets back up to a completely different scene in front of him.

He’s in a church, watching two people at the altar recite their wedding vows to each other. Tess and Wells get married and get ready to throw their bouquet into the crowd.

“When did you all get here?”

People gather into a group, hoping to catch the flowers. No one responds to Danny’s question, but Wells looks at Danny with a laugh, and throws the bouquet towards him. He catches the flowers, and briefly wonders if pizza and flowers would be good for a date with Valerie – but maybe not flowers like these, that might appear too forward.

The flowers wilt in his hand, and the scene changes in front of him. There’s the burning wreckage of a car crash, Wells crying over his wife’s death.

Danny knows this; he knows that Harrison Wells’s wife, Tess Morgan, was killed in a car crash 7 years ago. He knows that Wells was severely injured in the same car crash, though it didn’t lead to paralysis like his injuries in the particle accelerator explosion did. What he doesn’t know is why the Yellow Flash appears in this memory. The yellow speedster proceeds to stab Harrison Wells in the heart.

A ghost in a lab coat appears next to Danny, watching the scene unfold in front of him.

“Sorry you had to see that,” He admitted. “I don’t get visitors often.”

The same ghost from– from outside the ghost bubble, Danny suddenly remembered.

“This is inside? Inside the bubble?” he asked.

“Yes, it seems to replay core memories of mine. I’ve been trying to research it, figure out how it came to be or if I can make more,”

…Ghost bubbles…

“Since it’s going through my memories, it’s easy for me to get in and out. I’m not sure about other ghosts though, since I don’t get any visitors.”

…made of semi solid ectoplasm that were neurological imprints of once living beings… fuck…

“I should be able to get you out easily though.”

…his parents were often wrong about ghost stuff, but were they– were they actually right about this thing? They’d never even seen a ghost bubble before, but this fit their paradigm, their definition of “ghosts”, even if he – and some of the other ghosts – didn’t.

But, it’s not like the ecto-bubble hurt anybody (intentionally) but it wasn’t the same type of ‘ghost’ that Danny and most other ghosts were either.

“I’m sorry, I didn’t introduce myself, I’m Harrison Wells,” the lab coat ghost said. “And you are–?”

Danny gathered as much – he was inside an ecto-bubble within Harrison Wells’s lair, so the only other self aware ghost must be him.

“I’m… Danny Phantom,” he responded.

So those were real memories and it had some people he knew, but those memories didn’t answer anything; this is worse than any riddle Pandora could have come up with.

Was this Wells the one that Danny knew?

“You don’t happen to know of any species of nearly extinct animals in the Amity Park Zoo, do you?” Danny asked.

“I’m sorry, Biology isn’t my field of study,” Labcoat-Wells responded.

So, not his Wells then. It was the wrong answer to the ‘code phrase’. But what could that mean?

“Sorry, I’m just– I thought– I’m so confused!” Danny pointed back to the echo of the memory playing out in front of them. “W-Why did that guy–?”

Was this an alternate version of history playing out in front of him?

“I wish I knew,” Labcoat-Wells stared on as Yellow Flash from the memory pulls his hand out of Memory-Wells’s body, and pulls back his mask. The yellow speedster’s face begins to morph into that of Harrison Wells. That shouldn’t have been possible – the Yellow Flash didn’t even exist prior to the particle accelerator explosion, this –

“I mean, he just lived as me and ended up doing everything I was going to do anyway.” Labcoat-Wells mused. “Why go through all that work? To steal glory? Why not live with me instead of as me?”

Wells-Flash looked up. At Danny? Towards Danny? Was he part of the memory-echo, or was he like Danny and Labcoat-Wells, from outside the bubble?

He didn’t wait to find out; he pressed a button on his amulet and one breath later he was back in Pandora’s island, right in front of the keyboard and floating display that he’d left. Danny gasped for breath, trembling from fear.

“What did you see?” Pandora asked.

“I –he was –! I don’t understand!”

“Most riddles are like that,” Pandora responded.

Danny breathed in gulps of air, reminding himself he was no longer there.

“But I thought –?” He sighed. Last time he’d come here, Pandora’s riddles were obstacles instead of cryptic non answers. She helped him try to find the answers to his own questions – but that led to even more questions.

“Why’d you let me go so easily? Without any riddle?” Danny finally asked.

“Because I was asked to do so,”

Danny was surprised to get a real answer this time, but knowing Pandora, it would only lead to more frustrating questions.

“By who?” And maybe, this was the part of the ‘riddle’ where he had to connect dots. “Was it the same pers– the same ghost who took Eragon’s amulet out of your storage?”

“Yes,”

If they asked Pandora to let Danny use artifacts under her care, if they took an artifact that Danny had asked Pandora to store… well, they probably didn’t have to steal Eragon’s necklace from Pandora – it was probably given to them. Someone who was watching Danny’s actions. Did he have a stalker? Was it Vlad? Or someone else?

“Who are they? And why–?”

“I’ll tell you again what I told you before,” Pandora interrupted. “You will meet him soon, though from his perspective, the meeting has already happened.”

Who is this ghost that has Pandora’s cooperation? What are their goals? And how does Danny’s use of the ghost searching artifact help achieve those goals?

Another, more pressing, question overwhelmed his mind.

Just who was Dr. Harrison Wells? And who – or what – was the Yellow Flash?
