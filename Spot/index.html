Akumatization

This article is about the Paris phenomena. For specific key players, see Hawkmoth (Paris villain), Ladybug (Paris hero), Cat Noir (Paris hero), or List of known Miraculous


One artist's render of what an Akuma may look like, before merging with an object

Akumatization refers to the process by which an akuma transforms a civilian into a superpowered being – complete with costumed accessories and an alias. Akumatized people are typically considered ‘villains’ who are brainwashed into the service of Hawkmoth[1]. The only way to free a person from akumatization is to break the object where the akuma entered[2], and for the akuma to be purified by Ladybug[3]. A person can be akumatized for as short as a few minutes, or as long as several days (citation needed). Akumatized people have reported missing memories during their time brainwashed – due to this, and the fact that property damage is typically undone with the help of Ladybug, any crimes committed by an akumatized person is not punishable by law[4]. Many formerly akumatized people remember having psychic conversations with Hawkmoth.

History

While akumas have only become more well known to the public thanks to social media, it is speculated that people have been akumatized many a times in the past. It is known that akumas are created thanks to the power of the Butterfly miraculous. Ladybug, the holder of the Ladybug miraculous, and Cat Noir, the holder of the Cat miraculous, are frequent targets of akumatized person’s. It is believed this is because Hawkmoth desires their miraculous. In the modern day, akumatization has been geographically limited to Paris. Archeologists speculate that the Ladybug and Cat miraculouses have been used by notable revolutionaries through out history (citation needed).
